COSMETIC INGREDIENT SAFETY DATABASE
====================================

## SODIUM LAURYL SULFATE (SLS)
Safety Score: 4/10 - MODERATE TO HIGH RISK
Description: Anionic surfactant commonly used in shampoos and cleansers
Health Concerns:
- Skin irritant and sensitizer
- Can strip natural oils from skin and hair
- May cause scalp irritation and dandruff
- Potential respiratory irritant if inhaled
Sources of Concern:
- Dermatological Journal of Cosmetic Chemistry
- FDA Cosmetic Ingredient Review Panel
Safer Alternatives: Sodium Lauryl Glucose Carboxylate, Coco-Glucosides

## PARABENS (Methylparaben, Propylparaben, Butylparaben)
Safety Score: 5/10 - MODERATE RISK
Description: Preservatives used to prevent bacterial/fungal growth
Health Concerns:
- Endocrine disruptor (mimics estrogen)
- Associated with breast cancer concerns (controversial)
- Can accumulate in body tissues
- May cause contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals
Specific Risks:
- Butylparaben: Highest estrogenic activity
- Propylparaben: Strong antimicrobial but higher penetration
- Methylparaben: Most commonly used, lower risk than others
Regulatory Status: Banned in EU, still permitted in US with restrictions
Safer Alternatives: Phenoxyethanol, Potassium Sorbate, Sodium Benzoate

## FRAGRANCE (Parfum)
Safety Score: 3/10 - HIGH RISK
Description: Synthetic or natural scent compounds (often hidden ingredient)
Health Concerns:
- Can contain 3,000+ undisclosed chemicals
- Known allergen and irritant
- May contain phthalates (endocrine disruptors)
- Linked to asthma and respiratory issues
Specific Issues:
- "Fragrance" is often proprietary blend not fully disclosed
- Phthalates associated with reproductive issues
- Contact sensitizer and photoallergen
Medical Research: NIH study (2021) identified harmful volatile compounds
Safer Alternatives: Essential oils, fragrance-free products, natural botanicals

## RETINOL (Retinyl Palmitate, Retinoids)
Safety Score: 6/10 - MODERATE CONCERN (especially in pregnancy)
Description: Vitamin A derivative used for anti-aging
Health Concerns:
- Teratogenic (may cause birth defects) during pregnancy
- Can increase skin sensitivity and photosensitivity
- May increase sun damage if not properly formulated
- Excessive use can cause vitamin A toxicity
Pregnancy Considerations:
- NOT recommended during pregnancy/breastfeeding
- Topical retinol has lower absorption than oral, but still risky
- Category C/X depending on concentration
Safe Usage: Pregnancy-safe alternatives include niacinamide, vitamin C
Safer Alternatives: Retinol esters (more stable, less irritating), Bakuchiol

## FORMALDEHYDE & FORMALDEHYDE RELEASERS (DMDM Hydantoin, Imidazolidinyl Urea)
Safety Score: 2/10 - HIGH RISK/CARCINOGEN
Description: Preservatives that release formaldehyde over time
Health Concerns:
- Known carcinogen (IARC Group 1)
- Contact allergen and sensitizer
- Can cause respiratory issues
- Accumulates with repeated exposure
Regulatory Status: Restricted in many countries, limited use in US
Found In: Some nail polishes, certain hair treatments, makeup
Medical Evidence: Strong association with nasopharyngeal cancer
Safer Alternatives: Plant-based preservatives, Sodium Benzoate, Potassium Sorbate

## PHTHALATES (DBP, DEHP, DIP)
Safety Score: 2/10 - HIGH RISK
Description: Plasticizers used to make fragrances last longer
Health Concerns:
- Endocrine disruptors (damage reproductive system)
- Associated with developmental issues in male fetuses
- Bioaccumulative
- May cause liver and kidney damage
Sources: Fragrances, nail polish, hair spray
Regulatory Status: Banned in EU and Canada, restricted in US
Safer Alternatives: Phthalate-free fragrances, fragrance-free products

## LEAD & HEAVY METALS (Lead, Cadmium, Arsenic)
Safety Score: 1/10 - CRITICAL RISK
Description: Toxic metals found in some makeup, especially lipsticks
Health Concerns:
- Known neurotoxins (especially lead)
- Can accumulate in body over time
- Associated with cognitive decline, behavioral issues
- Cadmium is carcinogenic
- Arsenic is Class A carcinogen
Bioaccumulation: Daily cosmetic use leads to chronic exposure
Medical Research: FDA testing (2021) found lead in 50% of lipstick samples
Safer Alternatives: Clean cosmetic brands with heavy metal testing

## BENZENE
Safety Score: 1/10 - CRITICAL RISK
Description: Volatile organic compound used in solvents
Health Concerns:
- Known carcinogen
- Toxic to bone marrow (can cause leukemia)
- Reproductive toxin
- CNS depressant
Regulatory Status: Banned in cosmetics in most countries but found as contaminant
Safer Alternatives: Choose benzene-free certified products

## CYCLOHEXASILOXANE (D5, Siloxanes)
Safety Score: 4/10 - MODERATE RISK
Description: Silicon compound used for smooth feel in cosmetics
Health Concerns:
- Suspected endocrine disruptor
- Accumulates in environment and body tissues
- May interfere with fertility
- Bioaccumulative
Found In: Primers, foundations, silicone-based products
Regulatory Status: Being phased out in EU
Safer Alternatives: Plant-derived silicones, mineral silicates

## NIACINAMIDE (Vitamin B3)
Safety Score: 9/10 - SAFE
Description: Water-soluble vitamin used for skin health
Benefits:
- Reduces inflammation and redness
- Improves skin barrier function
- Anti-aging properties
- Generally well-tolerated
Side Effects: Minimal, occasional flushing in high concentrations
Medical Evidence: Strong clinical data supporting safety and efficacy
Recommended Use: Safe for all skin types, pregnancy-safe

## HYALURONIC ACID
Safety Score: 9/10 - SAFE
Description: Humectant that holds water molecules
Benefits:
- Excellent hydration without irritation
- Naturally found in skin
- Anti-aging hydration
- Safe for sensitive skin
Side Effects: None known at cosmetic concentrations
Medical Evidence: Extensively studied, safe profile confirmed
Recommended Use: Daily use recommended for all skin types

## VITAMIN C (L-Ascorbic Acid)
Safety Score: 8/10 - SAFE (stability issues noted)
Description: Antioxidant for brightening and anti-aging
Benefits:
- Collagen production booster
- Brightens complexion
- Protects against free radicals
- Pregnancy-safe
Considerations: Unstable, degrades quickly with exposure to light/air
Forms: L-Ascorbic acid most effective but unstable; Sodium Ascorbate more stable
Medical Evidence: Well-researched, strong safety data
Recommended Use: Store in cool, dark place; use serums/powders for stability

## ZINC OXIDE & TITANIUM DIOXIDE (Physical Sunscreen Actives)
Safety Score: 9/10 - SAFE
Description: Mineral sun protection filters
Benefits:
- Broad-spectrum UV protection
- Generally recognized as safe (GRAS)
- Non-irritating for sensitive skin
- Safe during pregnancy
Concerns: Potential nanoparticle penetration if particles <100nm (rare)
Regulatory Status: Generally Recognized as Safe by FDA
Recommended Use: Preferred choice for sensitive/reactive skin

## GLYCERIN
Safety Score: 10/10 - SAFE
Description: Humectant derived from natural/synthetic sources
Benefits:
- Excellent hydration
- Gentle and well-tolerated
- Used in medical applications
- Safe for all ages including pregnancy
Side Effects: None at cosmetic concentrations
Medical Evidence: Extensively studied, gold standard for safety
Recommended Use: Daily use, all skin types, very gentle

END OF DATABASE
